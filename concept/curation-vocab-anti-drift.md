# Operational Safeguards: Curation, Vocabulary, and Anti-Drift Rules

This document ensures the system remains executable, consistent, and protected from long-term degradation.

It complements:
- North Star
- Asset Model
- Tagging Standard

This is the **execution and protection layer**.

---

# 1. Curation Operating Standard

## Purpose

Ensure every new extraction is processed consistently, quickly, and without overthinking.

Curation must remain:
- fast
- repeatable
- low-friction
- decision-focused

---

## Core Rule

Every extracted document is a **review package**, not an automatic addition.

---

## Curation Flow

### Step 1 — Scan for Signal
Read only high-value sections:
- Foundations
- Patterns
- Decision Playbook

Goal:
Identify reusable, decision-relevant ideas.

---

### Step 2 — Identify Candidate Assets

Ask:
- Is this reusable?
- Is this decision-useful?
- Is this distinct?

If no → stop (do not promote)

---

### Step 3 — Classify

Each candidate must be classified as:

- New  
- Refinement  
- Duplicate  

---

### Step 4 — Assign Status

- Canonical  
- Supporting  
- Archive  
- Superseded  

Use strict promotion rules (defined in North Star).

---

### Step 5 — Concept Family Assignment

- Attach to existing concept family OR
- Create new one if truly distinct

---

### Step 6 — Apply Tags

- Domain  
- Type  
- Concept  
- Priority  
- Status  

Only after classification and status.

---

### Step 7 — Route

- Place in correct domain notebook

---

### Step 8 — Stop

Do not iterate endlessly.
Do not refine unnecessarily.

---

## Time Constraint

Target:

- ~30–60 seconds per asset decision

If longer:
- system is becoming too complex

---

## Decision Shortcut

If unsure:

- not clearly reusable → archive  
- not clearly better → do not promote  
- not clearly distinct → treat as refinement or duplicate  

---

# 2. Concept Naming & Vocabulary Standard

## Purpose

Prevent synonym drift and fragmentation of ideas over time.

This is one of the highest long-term risks.

---

## Core Rule

Every concept family must have a **stable, reusable name**.

---

## Naming Principles

### 1. Reusable
Must apply beyond one project or context.

### 2. Clear
Should immediately signal what the concept is about.

### 3. Concise
Avoid long or overly detailed names.

### 4. Stable
Once established, do not rename casually.

---

## Naming Format

Use one of:

- "[Concept] Principle"
- "[Concept] Pattern"
- "[Concept] Rule"
- "[Concept] Strategy"

Or:

- concise descriptive phrase

---

## Examples

Good:
- Extraction vs Curation Separation  
- Constraint-First Design  
- Selective Promotion Strategy  

Bad:
- Prompt Improvement Idea  
- Better Way of Structuring Things  
- Project Alpha Optimization  

---

## Synonym Control Rule

Before creating a new concept:

Ask:

- Does an existing concept cover 80% of this?
- Is this truly new, or just a better articulation?

If overlap exists:
→ merge into existing concept family

---

## Vocabulary Growth Rule

Only add new concepts when:

- clearly distinct  
- reusable  
- cannot be merged  

---

## Vocabulary Review (Occasional)

Periodically:

- scan concept names  
- merge overlaps  
- remove redundancy  

Do NOT over-maintain.

---

# 3. System Boundaries & Anti-Drift Rules

## Purpose

Protect the system from gradual complexity and noise.

Most systems fail not from bad design, but from slow drift.

---

## Boundary 1: Do Not Expand Scope

Do not turn this into:

- a full knowledge management system  
- a documentation system  
- a project tracking system  

It is a **decision support system only**.

---

## Boundary 2: Do Not Add Asset Types

Allowed:
- Foundation  
- Pattern  
- Case  
- Decision  

Do not add more unless absolutely necessary.

---

## Boundary 3: Do Not Expand Tag Model

Tag categories are fixed:

- Domain  
- Type  
- Concept  
- Priority  
- Status  

If tagging feels insufficient:
→ fix curation or canonical structure, not tags

---

## Boundary 4: Do Not Store Everything

This is the most important boundary.

If violated:
- system becomes noisy
- retrieval degrades
- trust collapses

---

## Boundary 5: Do Not Automate Critical Decisions

AI may assist, but must not decide:

- canonical promotion  
- concept merging  
- final inclusion  

---

## Boundary 6: Do Not Over-Split

Atomic review is good.

Atomic storage of everything is not.

---

## Boundary 7: Do Not Let Supporting Assets Multiply

Supporting assets must remain:

- few  
- high value  
- clearly useful  

---

## Boundary 8: Do Not Allow Multiple Canonicals

If two assets feel canonical:
→ merge or choose one

---

## Boundary 9: Do Not Let Archive Become Active

Archive is:

- inactive  
- not for regular retrieval  

If archive starts being used often:
→ something is wrong with promotion or canonical quality

---

## Boundary 10: Do Not Over-Optimize Early

Avoid:

- scoring systems  
- complex automation  
- heavy governance  
- perfect structure  

The system should evolve from real usage, not anticipation.

---

# 4. Drift Detection Signals

Watch for:

- too many assets being promoted  
- repeated concepts showing up  
- growing tag list  
- hesitation during curation  
- retrieval returning similar results  
- uncertainty about where something belongs  

---

# 5. Correction Playbook

If drift occurs:

### Too much noise
→ tighten promotion criteria  
→ archive aggressively  

### Duplicate concepts
→ merge concept families  
→ enforce canonical  

### Tag inconsistency
→ consolidate vocabulary  
→ remove synonyms  

### Curation fatigue
→ simplify decisions  
→ reduce volume  

---

# 6. Final Operating Principle

This system works because it is:

- selective  
- disciplined  
- simple  
- concept-driven  

It will fail if it becomes:

- inclusive  
- complex  
- fragmented  
- document-driven  

---

# 7. Summary

This document ensures:

- curation stays fast and consistent  
- concept vocabulary stays clean  
- system boundaries are protected  
- long-term drift is prevented  

## Final Standard

The system should always move toward:

- fewer, stronger concepts  
- cleaner canonicals  
- minimal noise  
- higher trust  
- easier decisions  

If that is happening, the system is working.
